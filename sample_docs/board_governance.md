# ESG Board Governance Metrics

Strong governance is a core ESG pillar. Common governance metrics include:

- Board independence percentage: proportion of non-executive independent
  directors. Typical leading-practice threshold is 50% or higher for listed
  entities.
- Female employee ratio and board gender diversity: many frameworks recommend
  at least 30% representation of the underrepresented gender on the board.
- Incident count: number of reportable compliance or ethics incidents in the
  fiscal year, including whistleblower cases, bribery, and data breaches.
- Audit committee independence and financial expertise.

Under CSRD and ESRS G1 (Business Conduct), companies must disclose their
policies on corporate culture, whistleblower protection, anti-corruption,
and animal welfare where relevant.
