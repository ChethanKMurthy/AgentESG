# Greenhouse Gas Emissions Scopes

The GHG Protocol classifies emissions into three scopes:

## Scope 1 — Direct Emissions

Scope 1 covers direct greenhouse gas emissions from sources owned or controlled
by the reporting company. Examples include fuel combustion in company boilers,
furnaces, vehicles, and emissions from chemical production. Units are
tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (tCO2e).

## Scope 2 — Indirect Emissions from Purchased Energy

Scope 2 covers indirect emissions from the generation of purchased electricity,
steam, heating, and cooling consumed by the reporting company. Two accounting
methods exist: location-based (grid average) and market-based (contractual
instruments such as RECs and PPAs).

## Scope 3 — Other Indirect Emissions

Scope 3 includes all other indirect emissions that occur in a company's value
chain, both upstream and downstream. The GHG Protocol defines 15 Scope 3
categories, including purchased goods and services, capital goods, business
travel, employee commuting, use of sold products, and end-of-life treatment.
Scope 3 typically represents the largest share of a company's total footprint.
